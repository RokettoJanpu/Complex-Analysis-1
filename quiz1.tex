\tbf{Note.} Define $\cis\theta:=\cos\theta+i\sin\theta$.
\sep



Solve $(z-1)^6=z^6$.\\

\ans We know $z\ne1$ since plugging in $z=1$ gives $0=1$. So
$$\br{\frac{z}{z-1}}^6 = 1 = \cis(2\pi k),~k\in\bZ
\imp \frac{z}{z-1} = \cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}},~1\le k\le 5$$
Taking the 6th root, and the condition $z\ne1$ means we take $1\le k\le 5$. Then
$$z = z\cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}} - \cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}}
\imp z\sbr{\cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}}-1} = \cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}}
\imp z = \frac{\cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}}}{\cis\br{\frac{\pi k}{3}}-1}$$
Plugging in the values of $k$ gives
$$z = \frac12,~\frac12\br{1\pm\frac{i}{\sqrt3}},~\frac12(1\pm i\sqrt3)$$
